Definition 1.5.2Total Catalyst Surface Area

The total catalyst surface area is:

\[ A = s_0 \left(1 + C \sum _{i=0}^{\infty } x^{i+1}\right) \]

Definition 1.7.1BET Equation (General Form)

The BET equation in its general form is:

\[ \text{BET} = \frac{V}{A \cdot V_0} \]
LaTeX Lean
Definition 1.2.1BET System Parameters

A BET system is characterized by:

  • \(s_0\) – surface area of zeroth layer (positive)

  • \(E_1\) – activation energy for first layer adsorption

  • \(E_L\) – activation energy for subsequent layers

  • \(R\) – universal gas constant (positive)

  • \(T\) – absolute temperature (positive)

  • \(a_1, b_1, g\) – fitted constants (positive)

  • \(P\) – pressure (positive)

  • \(P_0\) – saturation pressure (positive)

  • \(V_0\) – monolayer volume (positive)

LaTeX Lean
Definition 1.7.3BET Equation (Series Form)

The BET equation in series form is:

\[ \text{BET}_{\text{series}} = \frac{C \cdot s_0 \sum _{i=0}^{\infty } (i+1) \cdot x^{i+1}}{s_0(1 + C \sum _{i=0}^{\infty } x^{i+1})} \]
LaTeX Lean
Definition 1.7.2BET Equation (Simplified Form)

The simplified BET equation is:

\[ \text{BET}_{\text{simplified}} = \frac{C \cdot P}{(P_0 - P)(1 + (C-1) \cdot P/P_0)} \]
LaTeX Lean
Definition 1.4.3BET C Parameter

The BET constant relating first layer to subsequent layers is:

\[ C = \frac{y}{x} = \frac{C_1}{C_L} \]
LaTeX Lean
Definition 1.3.2First Layer Constant

The first layer equilibrium constant is:

\[ C_1 = \frac{a_1}{b_1} \exp (E_1 / (RT)) \]
LaTeX Lean
Definition 1.3.1Liquid Phase Constant

The liquid phase constant is:

\[ C_L = \frac{\exp (E_L / (RT))}{g} \]
LaTeX Lean
Definition 1.5.1Surface Area of i-th Layer

The surface area of the \(i\)-th adsorption layer is:

\[ s_i = \begin{cases} s_0 & \text{if } i = 0 \\ C \cdot x^i \cdot s_0 & \text{if } i \geq 1 \end{cases} \]

Definition 1.5.3Volume Adsorbed

The total volume adsorbed is:

\[ V = C \cdot s_0 \cdot V_0 \sum _{i=0}^{\infty } (i+1) \cdot x^{i+1} \]

Definition 1.4.1Dimensionless Pressure x

The dimensionless pressure parameter is:

Definition 1.4.2Dimensionless Pressure y

The first-layer dimensionless pressure is:

Lemma 1.8.1BET Main Mathematical Identity

For \(|x| {\lt} 1\) and \(C {\gt} 0\):

\[ \frac{C \sum _{i=0}^{\infty } (i+1) \cdot x^{i+1}}{1 + C \sum _{i=0}^{\infty } x^{i+1}} = \frac{C \cdot x}{(1-x)(1-x+Cx)} \]

Lemma 1.6.1Geometric Series Summability

For \(|x| {\lt} 1\), the series \(\sum _{n=0}^{\infty } x^{n+1}\) is summable.

LaTeX Lean
Lemma 1.6.3Simple Geometric Series Sum

For \(|x| {\lt} 1\):

\[ \sum _{n=0}^{\infty } x^{n+1} = \frac{x}{1-x} \]
LaTeX Lean
Lemma 1.6.2Weighted Geometric Series

For \(|x| {\lt} 1\):

\[ \sum _{n=0}^{\infty } (n+1) \cdot x^{n+1} = \frac{x}{(1-x)^2} \]

Theorem 1.9.1BET Derivation

Under the conditions:

  • All physical parameters positive

  • \(P {\lt} P_0\) (pressure below saturation)

  • \(|x| {\lt} 1\) (convergence condition)

  • \(P_0 = 1/C_L\) (saturation pressure relation)

The general BET equation equals the simplified form:

\[ \text{BET} = \text{BET}_{\text{simplified}} \]
LaTeX Lean
Theorem 1.9.2BET Series Equivalence

Under standard convergence conditions (\(|x| {\lt} 1\) and positive parameters), the general BET equation equals the series form:

\[ \text{BET} = \text{BET}_{\text{series}} \]
